This semester, specifically after being introduced to new AI agents like Claude and how to use GitHub Copilot in VS Code, it shows that coding is becoming something similar to math problems — to where once calculators came in you honestly didn't need to know how to do it. Once AI gets to the point where it doesn't make mistakes, I feel like humans are in a stage of life where we stop becoming human. We stop becoming stimulated by the dopamine of discovering something new or spending days solving a single problem. Yes, AI is more efficient, but losing what makes us human I believe will slowly halt human innovation as we will no longer have the creativity to create anymore. No execution skills, just ideas — and soon the ideas will also no longer be ours. I appreciate the technology, but knowing myself and other humans alike, I know we will abuse the power and in turn lose the essence of who we are in the process.

In future coursework I plan to continue to use AI the same way I have been, but overall continue to read and create more on my own. Push myself to go above and beyond even though I'm still using AI to cut corners — try to hold on as long as possible.
